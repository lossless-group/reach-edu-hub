# Faculty still doing the job they teach — half of them named Teacher or Principal of the Year in their home state

## Core messages

- "Nearly half of Reach's faculty are former teachers and principals of the year in their home states."
- Nearly 250 faculty and professors of practice university-wide.
- Faculty work full-time in classrooms alongside the candidates they teach — same challenges, same calendar, same students.
- 25:1 student-to-faculty ratio in seminars; learning is synchronous, not asynchronous video.
- Named exemplars include Dr. Cicely Woodard (Presidential Award; 2018 Tennessee Teacher of the Year) and Dr. Torrence Williams (2017 Louisiana High School Teacher of the Year).
- Pedagogical depth, not just credentials: culturally sustaining pedagogy, whole-child instruction, real K-12 caseloads.

## Verbatim quotes

> "Professors of practice still actively work in the field in which they teach."

> "We value professors who are immersed in their real-life work — because they make better teachers."

> "Learning at Reach is synchronous, offering a more engaging experience than many other remote-learning degrees."
