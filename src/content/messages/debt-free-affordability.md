# A bachelor's degree for no more than $75 a month, out-of-pocket, after financial aid

## Core messages

- B.A. in Liberal Studies and B.A. in Global Education both cap learner cost at "no more than $75/month" after aid.
- Candidates earn a paycheck while earning the degree — they build a resume instead of a debt load.
- "Nobody should be forced to choose between a job and a degree."
- Affordability is structural, not promotional: the model only works because employers and the workplace carry the practicum.
- Apprenticeship degrees "reduce the cost of higher education" while solving real workforce gaps.
- Caveat for graduate audiences: Reach scholarships do not currently extend to graduate programs — graduate affordability routes through employer and county-grant pathways.
