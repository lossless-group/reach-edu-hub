Operating Strategy · Small-Team Leverage
Operate like a team several times your size — and stop triaging away the high-leverage work.
Two effects, not one. Agents reduce overwhelm on work the team already does (the inbox, the follow-ups, the prep), and they enable work that wasn't possible at the team's size — ten outreach threads at once, conference outreach nine months ahead, a grant corpus that drafts itself. The discipline that makes it safe: humans stay on anything that goes outward.
  • Reduce overwhelm AND enable the impossible-at-your-size. The win isn't "the same work faster" — it's the long-horizon, high-leverage work a busy team always drops.
  • Humans on anything outward. Sent, published, submitted stays human-approved; agents research, draft, schedule, monitor, prepare — people approve and act.
  • Build the corpus + brand voice once, reuse everywhere. Grants, outreach, social, and event copy all draw from one source of truth.
01 Sales / ABM & Travel
  • ABM: prospect research, enrichment, sequencing, reply-triage — one person tends many threads.
  • Travel: build the itinerary, fill meetings around the dates, optimize drive-time, queue follow-ups.
  • Tools: Clay, Apollo, 11x; Badger/Circuit; n8n.
02 Events — 9 Months Ahead

Events reward people who start absurdly early. Agents track CFP deadlines, run speaker/sponsor outreach 9+ months out, scrape attendee lists, place pitches, set meetings, and generate per-meeting prep briefs — exactly the long-horizon work a busy team drops. Tools: Apollo/Instantly, Fireflies, Firecrawl/Apify, n8n.

03 Grants & Social
  • Grants: a reusable corpus + snippet library that drafts applications, form fill-outs, and DDQs against the funder's prompts.
  • Social: monitor stakeholders, surface timely opportunities, draft — a human posts (respect platform ToS).
04 The Guardrails
  • Humans on anything outward.
  • Start with one bottleneck; prove it, then expand.
  • Keep asset libraries agents pull from, so output is on-brand.
  • Measure both axes — time saved and work newly possible.
Augment, Don't Abdicate
~89.7% of social teams use AI daily but only ~5.4% fully automate — the data backs human-in-the-loop. Agents reduce overwhelm and enable the impossible-at-your-size; people stay on the action. Pick the right altitude — no-code glue (n8n/Zapier) → custom frameworks (LangGraph/CrewAI) → vertical tools (11x, Grantable) — and start with one bottleneck.
