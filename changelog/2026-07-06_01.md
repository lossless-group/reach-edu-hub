# One-pagers: the sixty-second version of every strategy

## Why Care?

A full strategy deck is the right tool for a forty-minute conversation.
A one-pager is the right tool for everything else — the cold email attachment,
the folder a funder's program officer prints before a committee meeting, the
leave-behind after a partner call.

All six strategy decks now have both. The Strategies menu surfaces the
one-pager directly from the card — no hunting through a drive or asking
someone to forward the PDF.

## What's New?

- **1-Pager pill on every strategy card** — a small, teal-outlined badge sits
  at the top-right of each card on `/strategies`, next to the Draft status chip.
  Clicking it navigates to the one-pager wrapper; clicking anywhere else on the
  card still opens the full deck.
- **Wrapper page at `/one-pager/[deck]/`** — a clean full-height view with a
  dark topbar showing a back-link to Strategies, the deck title, and an orange
  **Download PDF** button. The one-pager HTML renders in an iframe below.
- **Six one-pagers published** — Rural Income, Literacy & Numeracy, NCAD Forge,
  Agent Workflow Maxxing, Reverse Engineer Available Funding, and Frontier Job
  Demand. Each ships as both a styled HTML brief and a PDF.
- **`onePager` flag in the deck registry** — `decks.ts` now carries
  `onePager?: boolean` on each deck. `collections.ts` resolves it into
  `onePagerHref` on the `CollectionDeck` type, keeping the menu component
  declarative rather than slug-matching by hand.

## How it Works

The card layout uses the **block-link overlay pattern**: the card wrapper is a
`<div class="relative">`, a transparent `<a class="absolute inset-0">` covers
the whole card for deck navigation, and the 1-Pager `<a class="relative z-10">`
sits above it. This avoids nesting `<a>` inside `<a>` (invalid HTML that breaks
the grid layout) while keeping the whole card clickable.

The one-pager HTML files are self-contained — they carry their own styles and
fonts — so they live in `public/one-pagers/<slug>/index.html` and are served
as static assets. The wrapper page iframes them with an explicit `index.html`
suffix (Astro's dev server does not resolve directory-index paths for `public/`
files without it).
