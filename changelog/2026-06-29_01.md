# Reach joins the shell — and grows a layer above single decks

## Why Care?

Until today reach-edu-hub was a standalone site: its own `PageAsDeckWrapper`, its own variant registry, its own deck chrome — none of it shared with the other DidiDecks client-sites. Every improvement to the shared shell (the review TOC, in-deck slide ranking, the play runtime) passed reach by.

reach now runs on `@dididecks/shell` like chroma, humain, and lossless. The three decks — the Reach Story (baseline + editorial) and Pipeline & Automation — moved onto the shell's `/scroll/[deck]/[variant]` convention, so they inherit the shared chrome and show up in the review tooling. Old links (`/story`, `/story/version-2`, `/automation`) redirect to the new routes, so nothing already shared with Reach breaks.

Then we added something the shell didn't have: a **menu layer above single decks**. The whole DidiDecks workflow so far has been about converging on *one* deck through variants. Reach needs to present *many* distinct decks — different narratives, each a "strategy." So there are now three **collections** — Stories, Automations, Strategies — each with its own menu, grouping peer decks without touching their URLs.

And we proved the strategy loop end-to-end with the first one: **Rural Income Boosts through Apprenticeship Degrees** — a narrative authored in markdown, then designed into a full Scroll-UI deck.

## What's New?

**On the shell.** reach-edu-hub joined the workspace and added the `dididecksShell({...})` integration. Output flipped to `server` (the shell's injected routes run esbuild during prerender, which a static build can't do). The three decks live at `/scroll/story/baseline`, `/scroll/story/editorial`, and `/scroll/automation/pipeline`; `/toc/...` review pages and the in-deck rank pill work. All 29 section components gained `data-slot` / `data-variant` annotations so the rank pill tracks the active slot.

**Deck collections.** A new `collections.ts` registry plus a `DeckCollectionMenu` component and three menu routes — `/stories`, `/automations`, `/strategies`. Collections reference decks by slug; they don't rename decks or appear in deck URLs. Strategy narratives are authored as folders under `context-v/narratives/strategies/<name>/`. The full concept (collection vs. variant — breadth vs. depth) is written up in `context-v/explorations/Deck-Collections-A-Menu-Layer-Above-Single-Deck-Convergence.md`.

**First Strategy deck — Rural Income.** Eleven Scroll-UI slides arguing the Apprenticeship Degree as a rural economic-development engine — income that rises and stays local — including a national/regional/local **supporter & funder pipeline** slide. Live at `/scroll/rural-income/v1`, listed on `/strategies`. The draft narrative + cited sources live at `context-v/narratives/strategies/rural-income/README.md`; unverified figures are flagged in-deck with a `VERIFY` marker.

**A token-driven design system (the real upgrade).** New `src/styles/deck-primitives.css` — a reusable, mode-aware section vocabulary (`.deck-section`, `.deck-h1/h2`, `.deck-gradient`, `.deck-card`, `.deck-stat`, `.deck-chip`, `.deck-glow`). It reads only the Reach brand's Tier-2 semantic + `--fx-*` tokens (including the per-mode headline gradient and card shadow the older sections ignored), so light / dark / vibrant work with zero per-element variant classes. This is the drag-and-drop base future strategy decks build on — coherent without being frozen.

## What's Next?

- Verify the rural-income figures (wage delta, grant amounts, NCAD target) and fill the regional/local funder tiers with real names.
- Wire the three collection menus into the site nav.
- Build out the rest of the strategies.
- Upstream candidates for `@dididecks/shell`: `DeckCollectionMenu`, the `collections.ts` shape, and `deck-primitives.css`. Also a shell fix so variant slugs can repeat across decks (today they must be globally unique).
