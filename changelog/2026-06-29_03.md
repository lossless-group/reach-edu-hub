# Six strategies, one menu, now in the nav

## Why Care?

The `strategies` collection is the breadth layer above single-deck convergence —
a menu of distinct strategic narratives, each its own deck. It now holds **six**,
spanning Reach's funding case, its pedagogy, its operating model, and its program
design. **Strategies is now a top-nav item**, so the collection is reachable from
every page instead of by URL.

## What's New?

The six decks, each at `/scroll/<deck>/<variant>` and listed on `/strategies`:

1. **Rural Income Boosts through Apprenticeship Degrees** — the model as a
   place-based income engine.
2. **Improving Literacy & Numeracy in Working Adults** — lift (and measure) the
   foundational skills most tied to wages, inside the degree.
3. **NCAD Forge** — a cross-member agentic product lab to build the apprenticeship
   software stack.
4. **Agent Workflow Maxxing** — agent workflows so a small team operates like a
   big one (ABM, travel, events, grants, social).
5. **Reverse Engineer Available Funding** — funding follows a power law; study the
   biggest aligned funders and size the ask to their sweet spot.
6. **Opinionated Program Design: Frontier Job Demand** — design programs backward
   from demand: advanced manufacturing, drone sciences, AI in business operations.

**Navigation:** added **Strategies** to the hub's primary nav (between Story and
Automation), active across `/strategies` and the strategy deck pages.

All six are built on `deck-primitives.css` (token-driven; light/dark/vibrant work
with no per-element variant classes) and use grant-seeking / philanthropy framing.
Every borrowed stat is flagged in-deck with a `VERIFY` marker.

## What's Next?

Verify the `VERIFY`-flagged figures against primary sources before any deck goes
in front of funders; map each frontier field and funder pattern to specific NCAD
member employers and prospects; and iterate variants on the strongest decks
through the normal dididecks single-deck workflow.
