# Story 03 — The Care-Economy Workforce Emergency

## The thesis (the story Reach tells)

The U.S. is short *half* the behavioral-health workforce it will need by 2036, while one in
five adults lives with mental illness and nearly half go untreated. The roles exist, the
talent exists — behavioral health technicians, recovery coaches, community health workers,
case managers are all "apprenticeable" — but **no debt-free, work-based pathway from those
incumbent jobs to licensure existed.** Reach built it: the **Apprenticeship College of Health
(ACH)**, the nation's first, launched with the SEIU Healthcare 1199NW Training Fund in
Washington, starting in behavioral health. The ask: "the mental-health crisis is a *workforce*
crisis; fund the only proven engine that manufactures the missing workforce."

## Why it's credible (proof points)

- HRSA projects the nation will lack **half** the behavioral-health workforce needed by 2036;
  ~42,000 average annual openings through 2032; mental-health occupations growing faster than
  the 3% average.
  *(Source: `the-goodness-web-foundation/2026-06-11_announcement-1m-grant...`)*
- ACH is "a first-of-its-kind, partnership-driven apprenticeship degree program," launched in
  WA, "earning the support of leading mental health philanthropies including **Ballmer Group,
  The Goodness Web, and Accelerate the Future**." The Training Fund "founded the first
  Substance Use Disorder Professional registered apprenticeship program in the nation" and has
  partnered with 50+ employers.
  *(Source: `ballmer-group-ii/2026-06-11_reach-university-and-training-fund-launch-apprenticeship-col.md`)*
- Already-committed money in this lane: **The Goodness Web's 3-year $1M** behavioral-health
  grant to Reach.
  *(Source: Goodness Web announcement)*

## The funder cluster (who this resonates with, and why)

This is the densest, warmest cluster in the entire corpus — several funders are *already in*.

- **The Goodness Web Foundation** — already funding Reach ($1M, behavioral health, youth
  mental-health focus). Existing relationship → renewal/expansion + reference.
  *(Source: `the-goodness-web-foundation/`)*
- **Ballmer Group** — already a named ACH supporter; "historic initiative to invest in a
  strong, more diverse—and debt-relieved—behavioral health workforce." Strongly on-thesis.
  *(Source: `ballmer-group-ii/`)*
- **Anschutz Foundation** — "Transformational Gift to Mental Health"; "CU Anschutz Receives
  $50M Gift to Transform Mental Health Care." Big-check mental-health funder; Colorado is a
  Reach state.
  *(Source: `anschutz-foundation/`)*
- **Steven & Alexandra Cohen Foundation** — "$116M to LaGuardia Community College to create
  the Cohen Career Collective"; underserved-communities giving. Proven to write very large
  checks at the community-college/career intersection.
  *(Source: `steve-and-alexandra-cohen-fnd/`)*
- **Quell Foundation** — explicitly funds bridging "the Mental Health and Addiction Treatment
  Workforce Gap"; has a "Workforce Development Program." Near-perfect thematic fit.
  *(Source: `quell-foundation/`)*
- **The Jed Foundation (Todd Fisher)** — youth mental health, emotional well-being institute.
  Adjacency: pipeline of mental-health professionals for youth-serving settings.
  *(Source: `todd-fisher/`)*
- **Karl/Bette Rathjen Foundation** — emotional health (Dallas/TX). Smaller, in-footprint.
  *(Source: `karl-rathjen/`)*
- **Healthcare/nursing adjacency:** **Trellis** (nursing apprenticeship model w/ CHRISTUS,
  TX), **Hearst** (first-generation nursing students), **Lilly Endowment** (Ivy Tech early
  childhood workforce, $21.9M). Same "turn care jobs into credentials" mechanic, different
  profession.
  *(Source: `trellis-foundation/`, `hearst-foundation/`, `lilly-endowment/`)*

## The wedge / how to make the ask

**Reframe the crisis as a workforce problem with a manufacturing solution.** Mental-health
funders are used to funding *services and access*; Reach offers the rarer thing — *supply*.
For the already-in funders (Goodness Web, Ballmer), the ask is expansion + co-investment
recruitment ("bring two more funders to the table"). For the big-check mental-health funders
(Anschutz, Cohen), ACH is a *named, fundable institution* that turns their crisis dollars into
permanent local workforce. Use Reach's own line: talent "hidden in plain sight, ready to fill
critical professional roles with the right training and degree."

## What to verify before this goes to a funder

- Anschutz/Cohen alignment is inferred from gift *headlines*; confirm they fund *workforce
  pipelines* (not only facilities/research/services) via a body read of their giving pages.
- "Accelerate the Future" and the Smith Family Foundation's "Massachusetts Apprenticeship
  Degree Project" appear as ACH/NCAD partners — map whether they're in the pipeline CSV.
