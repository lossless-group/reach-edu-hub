# Story 02 — Teaching Already Proved It

## The thesis (the story Reach tells)

Every funder fears funding a good idea that never works in the field. Reach removes that fear:
the apprenticeship degree was *born* solving the teacher shortage, and it works — **420+ K-12
school systems across 8+ states**, paraprofessionals and school staff earning real degrees
while they keep working, paid by their employers, finishing with no debt. The teacher story is
both a fundable program *and* the **evidence base** that de-risks every other Reach expansion.
For education funders, the ask is: "the educator-pipeline fix you've been looking for is
already running at scale — help us deepen it and reach the next district."

## Why it's credible (proof points)

- "Reach currently partners with **420 K-12 school systems in eight states** to provide
  apprenticeship degree pathways for paraprofessionals and other school employees."
  *(Source: `the-goodness-web-foundation/2026-06-11_announcement-1m-grant-to-expand-apprenticeship-degrees-in-be.md`)*
- The model is explicitly the template the healthcare expansion is "modeled after the Reach
  Teachers College" — i.e., teaching is the validated original.
  *(Source: `ballmer-group-ii/2026-06-11_reach-university-and-training-fund-launch-apprenticeship-col.md`)*
- After grants/scholarships the full-time undergraduate out-of-pocket is **~$900/yr (~$75/mo),
  no loans** — the affordability claim that makes the teacher pipeline durable.
  *(Source: same Goodness Web announcement)*
- State machinery is already moving in Reach states: e.g., Alabama enacted programs and an
  Office of Apprenticeship / credentialing-and-career-pathways committee.
  *(Source: `alabama-state-legislature-appropriations-funds/`)*

## The funder cluster (who this resonates with, and why)

- **Charles & Lynn Schusterman Family Philanthropies** — "Building Strong Readers, Writers and
  Thinkers"; education/literacy. Teacher quality is upstream of literacy.
  *(Source: `schusterman-family-philanthropies/`)*
- **Walton Family Foundation** — career-connected learning ("Catalyze Awards $3.5M…"),
  education, talent pipelines; a major K-12 reform funder.
  *(Source: `walton-family-foundation/`)*
- **Morgridge Family Foundation** — students/education, "Future of Work Development | Workforce
  Skills," "Empowering The Next Generation." Family-foundation relationship potential.
  *(Source: `morgridge-foundation/`)*
- **Gates Foundation** — "Pathways Education Program," "A pathway from high school to college to
  career." The pathways frame maps directly onto teacher-pipeline pathways.
  *(Source: `the-gates-foundation/`)*
- **Greater Texas Foundation / Trellis** — Texas postsecondary + workforce alignment; Reach
  operates in TX, so these are *in-footprint* education funders.
  *(Source: `greater-texas-foundation/`, `trellis-foundation/`)*
- **Hyde Family Foundation** — Memphis education focus; in-footprint (TN) education giving.
  *(Source: `hyde-foundation/`)*

## The wedge / how to make the ask

This is the **"de-risk by track record"** story. For education funders skeptical of yet
another teacher-prep startup, lead with the scale numbers and the fact that *employers
(districts) pay the apprentices* — it's not a subsidy that disappears when the grant ends.
For in-footprint funders (TX, TN, CO, GA, AL), make it local: "here are the districts in
*your* state already doing this; fund the next ten."

## What to verify before this goes to a funder

- The "420 systems / 8 states" figure is from a Feb-2025 announcement; the ACH release lists
  10 operating states (adds MS, WA). Use the **most current** count — confirm against a recent
  Reach source before printing.
- Outcome data (completion rates, retention-in-teaching, student outcomes) would make this
  story much stronger and is *not* in the sampled files — pull it from Reach directly or a
  body-level read of the corpus.
