# Story 01 — The Apprenticeship Degree Is a New Category

## The thesis (the story Reach tells)

For a decade the field has circled the same question: *if college isn't working for working
adults, what replaces it?* The answers piled up — registered apprenticeships, work-based
learning, skills-based hiring, "learn and earn." But almost all of them stop *short* of a
degree. Reach closed the gap. The **apprenticeship degree** — accredited, work-embedded,
employer-paid, debt-free — is not a tweak to college; it is a **new category**, and Reach,
through the National Center for the Apprenticeship Degree (NCAD), is the only organization
running it at national scale across multiple professions. The ask isn't "fund our program."
It's "**fund the institution that's defining the category the whole field is moving toward.**"

## Why it's credible (proof points)

- Reach is described, in its own and partners' language, as "the nation's first and only
  nonprofit university fully dedicated to advancing on-the-job degrees." It "pioneered the
  U.S. teacher apprenticeship degree and ensured others can do the same" (NCAD already
  collaborating with TCNJ, Metro State Denver, and Mass Bay Community College).
  *(Source: `the-goodness-web-foundation/2026-06-11_announcement-1m-grant-to-expand-apprenticeship-degrees-in-be.md`)*
- The category language is spreading independently of Reach: the corpus contains field
  pieces titled *"Reimagining postsecondary pathways: Apprenticeship degrees"* (Community
  College Daily), *"Mapping the Landscape of Degree Apprenticeship: Expanding a Promising
  Model for Mobility,"* and *"Higher-education philanthropy: Why not apprenticeships?"* —
  evidence the *idea* has momentum the *instance* (Reach) can capture.
  *(Source: `inbox/` — multiple files)*

## The funder cluster (who this resonates with, and why)

- **Lumina Foundation** — the most on-thesis macro funder. Corpus shows its "75% of workers
  with higher education" goal, its shift to "ensure college grads gain higher incomes," and a
  podcast literally titled *"Apprenticeship 2.0 | Higher Education and Apprenticeships with
  Jamie Merisotis."* Lumina is publicly arguing for exactly Reach's category.
  *(Source: `lumina/` — `apprenticeship-2-0...`, `lumina-sets-new-goal-75...`)*
- **Jobs for the Future (JFF)** — field intermediary and funder-adjacent; corpus shows
  education-to-employment pathway work, AI-ready workforce reports, and the Fair Chance
  initiative. JFF is a credibility/co-sign partner as much as a funder.
  *(Source: `jff/`)*
- **Strada Education Foundation** — work-based-learning framework owner ("On-ramps to Good
  Jobs," "Scaling Work-Based Learning," the *Talent Disrupted* underemployment data). Strada
  funds the *evidence layer* the category needs.
  *(Source: `strada-education-foundation/`)*
- **Charles Koch Foundation / Stand Together** — "Beyond degrees," skills-based hiring,
  non-degree-holder hiring, "Skillability Index." Ideologically primed for an alternative to
  the traditional degree; a degree that *is* the alternative is a strong fit.
  *(Source: `charles-koch-foundation/`, `stand-together-trust/`)*
- **Hearst Foundations** — published *"Apprenticeships: A Donor's Guide to Creating New
  Pathways to Opportunity,"* arguing apprenticeship is "an area ripe for philanthropic
  impact." A funder that has already written the donor case to itself.
  *(Source: `hearst-foundation/2026-06-11_apprenticeships-a-donor-s-guide-to-creating-new-pathways-to.md`)*
- **ECMC Foundation** — postsecondary CTE, "States Hold the Keys to Unlocking Higher
  Education's Value," student-success recalibration. Funds the policy/value frame.
  *(Source: `ecmc-2-honorarium/`)*

## The wedge / how to make the ask

Lead with **category, not charity.** These funders are publishing thought leadership that
*describes the gap Reach fills* — so the ask is "you've named the future of higher ed; Reach
is the operating proof it exists; help us make it the default, not the exception." For Lumina
and Strada specifically, position NCAD as the **field infrastructure** (standards, the
"apprenticeable role → degree → licensure" pathway) — funders love funding the rails, not just
one train.

## What to verify before this goes to a funder

- Lumina/Strada/JFF are partly **intermediaries**; confirm which give direct grants vs. act
  as co-signers/conveners (a body read of their "focus areas" / "how to apply" pages).
- The "field is converging" claim rests on inbox think-pieces; confirm dates and authors so
  the momentum story is current (some inbox pieces date to 2014).
