# Story 06 — Talent Hidden in Plain Sight: Place-Based & Rural Pipelines

## The thesis (the story Reach tells)

Traditional higher ed extracts talent from communities — the best students leave for college
and often don't come back. Reach's model does the opposite: because the degree is embedded in
a *local job* and earned *in place*, the talent Reach develops **stays where it's needed** —
the rural school district, the regional health system, the small-town employer. For place-based
funders (community foundations, family foundations rooted in a state or city) and rural-focused
funders, that's the whole game: Reach is how you build a workforce that doesn't move away. The
ask: "fund the pipeline *in your region* — the talent is already living there."

## Why it's credible (proof points)

- Reach's mission language is explicitly place-rooted: degrees, credentials, and careers "in
  their home communities"; the model builds "community-based care" and a "qualified, local
  pipeline."
  *(Source: `ballmer-group-ii/2026-06-11_reach-university-and-training-fund-launch-apprenticeship-col.md`)*
- The talent is framed as local and latent — "a potential talent pool hidden in plain sight" —
  i.e., people already embedded in the community.
  *(Source: `the-goodness-web-foundation/2026-06-11_announcement-1m-grant...`)*
- Reach's operating footprint is concrete and largely non-coastal: AL, AR, CA, CO, GA, LA, MS,
  TX, TN, WA — strong overlap with many place-based funders' geographies.
  *(Source: same ACH release)*

## The funder cluster (who this resonates with, and why)

- **ZOMA Foundation (Zoma Lab)** — rural Colorado focus ("Rural Community Response and Recovery
  Project," outcomes financing in Colorado), early childhood. CO is a Reach state.
  *(Source: `zoma-foundation-of-zoma-lab/`)*
- **ECMC Foundation** — "Building Momentum for **Rural** Institutions and Learners"; explicit
  rural-postsecondary lens. *(Source: `ecmc-2-honorarium/`)*
- **Gates Family Foundation** — Denver/Colorado community development; "Senior Program Officer
  for Community Development." A *regional* funder (distinct from the global Gates Foundation).
  *(Source: `gates-family-foundation/`)*
- **Daniels Fund** — Colorado/regional grantmaker. In-footprint. *(Source: `daniels-fund/`)*
- **Greater Texas Foundation** — Texas postsecondary/workforce; in-footprint.
  *(Source: `greater-texas-foundation/`)*
- **Sobrato Philanthropies** — regional (Silicon Valley/CA) place-based giving.
  *(Source: `sobrato-philanthropies/`)*
- **S.H. Cowell Foundation** — California regional/community focus.
  *(Source: `s-h-cowell-foundation/`)*
- **Kenneth Rainin Foundation** — Oakland, CA; "Opportunity Build Program." Hyper-local.
  *(Source: `kenneth-rainin-foundation/`)*
- **The Denver Foundation / Caring for Denver** — Colorado community grants. (And note Denver/CO
  recurs across Zoma, Gates Family, Daniels, Anschutz → **Colorado is a corpus hotspot worth a
  dedicated regional push**.) *(Source: `the-denver-foundation-beacon/`)*

## The wedge / how to make the ask

**Make every ask geographic.** This is the story where you *don't* lead with the national
model — you lead with the map: "here are the districts/health systems in *your* state already
running Reach apprenticeship degrees; here's the local talent staying local." Colorado is the
strongest regional cluster (Zoma, Gates Family, Daniels, Anschutz, Denver Foundation) — worth a
purpose-built CO mini-deck. Rural is its own sub-angle for ECMC and USDA-adjacent funders.

## What to verify before this goes to a funder

- Place-based funders almost always have **strict geographic eligibility** — confirm Reach has
  active programs in each funder's required region before pitching (otherwise it's an instant
  decline).
- The Colorado density is a strong signal but partly inferred from funder HQ/geography; confirm
  each CO funder's *program* areas align (some may be early-childhood- or housing-only).
