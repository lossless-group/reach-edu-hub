# Improving Literacy & Numeracy in Working Adults

> **DRAFT — seed for deck design.** A `strategies`-collection narrative. Audience:
> philanthropy / grant funders (and Reach leadership). This engagement is
> grant-seeking, not a fundraise — framing stays in foundational-skills and
> mobility terms, not investment terms. **Figures below are flagged `⚠ VERIFY`**:
> they came from web snippets and must be confirmed against primary sources
> (PIAAC/NCES, Hanushek et al.) before this goes in front of anyone.

## The through-line

There is a quiet ceiling on adult economic mobility: **foundational skills**.
Decades of evidence — most cleanly the OECD's PIAAC survey of adult skills —
show that **literacy and numeracy independently predict earnings, employment,
and advancement**, *even after controlling* for years of schooling, credentials,
experience, and demographics. The skill itself is paid for, not just the diploma.

But two facts collide. First, a large share of working adults sit **below
proficiency** in exactly these skills — concentrated in the frontline and
service-sector workers who are the Apprenticeship Degree's core population.
Second, a degree program can hand someone a **credential without guaranteeing
the underlying basics actually improved** — capability and certification come
apart.

The strategy closes that gap. The pedagogy is finally ready: the **science of
reading** (the evidence base that reshaped K-12 literacy) is now reaching adult
education, and **contextualized, embedded numeracy** instruction has a growing
evidence base. Fold those into the Apprenticeship Degree — taught *in the flow of
work*, contextualized to the job — and **measure the gain** (PIAAC-style
pre/post). The result: the degree doesn't just certify; it durably lifts the
literacy and numeracy the labor market rewards, locking in the income and
promotion gains and making them real rather than nominal.

So the reframe for a funder: this is not remediation tacked onto a degree. It is
a **capability guarantee** — the assurance that an Apprenticeship Degree
graduate leaves measurably stronger in the two skills most tightly linked to
lifetime earnings.

## Why it moves income — the mechanism

1. **The skills are independently priced.** PIAAC wage analyses find a sizable
   earnings premium per standard deviation of numeracy/literacy, robust to
   controls for education. ⚠ VERIFY: Hanushek et al. (2015) report ~18% higher
   wages per SD of numeracy across countries on average, and notably higher in
   the U.S. (~28%) — confirm exact figures and framing.
2. **Promotions, not just starting wage.** Higher foundational skills track with
   advancement and on-the-job learning over the career, so the gain compounds —
   the lifecycle return is larger than early-career snapshots suggest. ⚠ VERIFY.
3. **Capability is durable.** Unlike a one-time credential signal, a real gain in
   reading/math travels with the worker across roles and employers.
4. **It de-risks the degree's promise.** Measured gains turn "you earned a
   degree" into "you can demonstrably do more" — which is what sustains the raise.

## The gap this fills

- **Most working adults are below proficiency.** ⚠ VERIFY: PIAAC 2023 (U.S.) —
  only ~38% of adults reached Level 3+ in numeracy and ~44% in literacy (so a
  majority are below Level 3 in numeracy). In 2017, ~19% of U.S. adults scored at
  or below Level 1 in literacy; tens of millions low-numeracy. OECD's latest
  survey flagged a *decline*.
- **The Apprenticeship Degree's population is the most affected.** ⚠ VERIFY: AIR
  analysis of service-sector workers found ~62% with limited literacy and ~74%
  with limited numeracy — precisely the frontline workers Reach serves.
- **Degrees can certify without lifting the basics.** Without explicit,
  measured foundational-skills instruction, the gain is assumed, not assured.

## The pedagogy is ready

- **Science of reading → adult education.** The validated evidence base that
  reshaped children's literacy (phonological awareness, decoding, fluency,
  vocabulary, comprehension as connected processes) is now being adapted for
  adult learners (e.g. ProLiteracy's work on the science of reading in adult
  education). ⚠ VERIFY specifics.
- **Contextualized / embedded numeracy.** Teaching math *in the context of the
  work and life tasks adults actually do* outperforms decontextualized drill;
  IES adult-education evidence reviews catalog promising strategies. ⚠ VERIFY.
- **Work-embedded delivery fits the model.** The Apprenticeship Degree already
  teaches in the flow of work — the natural vehicle for contextualized
  foundational-skills instruction.

## Assure the gain — measurement

The differentiator is **assurance**: pre/post assessment (PIAAC-aligned or a
validated instrument) so every cohort's literacy/numeracy gain is *visible*. That
turns the strategy into a fundable, evaluable program with a clear outcome metric
(skill-level movement) sitting upstream of the mobility metric (wage/promotion).
