Operating Proposal · Shared Lab
Prototype with experts; harden with engineers.
The Apprenticeship Degree runs on a different technology stack — one that integrates work and learning, gives employers visibility, and delivers chunked, assessable learning to busy adults while meeting accreditation. Historically only expensive engineering teams could build it. As of 2026, agentic "vibe coding" lets the domain experts closest to the problem prototype it themselves. This is the proposal to stand that capability up as a shared lab at NCAD.
  • A different stack — and nobody built it. Legacy LMSs, university SISs, and online-program platforms each solve a slice; none solve the whole.
  • Who builds has changed. Vibe coding moved prototyping from the engineering team to the domain expert; engineers still own hardening, scale, and security.
  • One network, one lab. NCAD's member network pools prototypes so no member rebuilds what another already proved.
01 A Different Stack

The Apprenticeship Degree must integrate work and learning (the job is the curriculum), give employers visibility and a way to encourage apprentices, and deliver chunked learning + assessment to busy adults — all while satisfying credit-bearing accreditation. Incumbents built parts; the whole is unbuilt.

02 Who Builds Has Changed

"Vibe coding" — building software by describing intent to an AI — matured by 2026 into something a non-engineer can ship real prototypes with (Cursor, v0, Lovable, Bolt, Replit Agent, Claude Code). Engineers are no longer the gate on prototyping; the domain experts who know the problem now build it.

03 How the Lab Works
  • Domain experts prototype — working software in hours/days.
  • Test with real cohorts/employers inside member programs.
  • Circulate across members through NCAD.
  • Engineers harden what proves out.
04 Candidate First Builds
  • Work ↔ credit mapping (accreditation-legible).
  • Employer visibility & encouragement dashboard.
  • Chunked delivery + stackable assessment.
  • Voice-first evidence capture — capture in the moment; the commute becomes learning time.
Charter the Lab
Fund a small standing capability — domain-expert builders + part-time engineering + tool budget — hosted at NCAD, governed by and serving the member network. Grant-funded, shared-good output: shared infrastructure for the field, not a commercial bet. So no member rebuilds what another already proved.
